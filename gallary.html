Lion

Lion The lion is known as the “King of the Jungle” and is one of the most powerful predators in the wild. Unlike other big cats, lions live in groups called prides. Male lions are easily recognized by their thick manes. Lions mainly hunt large animals and live in African savannas and grasslands.

leopard

Leopard The leopard is a fast and powerful big cat known for its spotted coat. It is an excellent climber and often drags its prey into trees to protect it from other predators. Leopards are mostly solitary animals and are active during the night. They are adaptable and can live in forests, grasslands, and mountains.

Hippo

Hippopotamus The hippopotamus is a large, semi-aquatic mammal found mainly in rivers and lakes of Africa. Despite its bulky body and short legs, it is a strong swimmer and can move quickly in water. Hippos spend most of the day in water to keep cool and come out at night to graze on grass. They are herbivores but are known to be very aggressive when threatened.

Rhino

Rhinoceros The rhinoceros is a powerful land animal recognized by its thick skin and one or two horns on its nose. Rhinos live in grasslands and forests and feed mainly on grass, leaves, and shrubs. Although they have poor eyesight, their sense of smell and hearing is very strong. Many rhino species are endangered due to illegal poaching.

Zebra

Zebra The zebra is a wild animal famous for its black and white striped body. Each zebra has a unique stripe pattern, just like human fingerprints. Zebras live in groups called herds and are commonly found in African grasslands. They are herbivores and mainly feed on grass.

Elephant

Elephant The elephant is the largest land animal on Earth and is known for its intelligence and strong social bonds. It has a long trunk used for drinking water, picking food, and communication. Elephants live in herds and are found in Africa and Asia. They are herbivores and play an important role in maintaining the ecosystem.
